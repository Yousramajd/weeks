Infinity

by Giacomo Leopardi

It's always been dear to me, this isolated hill
And this hedge, that from vast parts
Of the farthest horizon excludes sight.
But sitting and gazing, interminable
Space beyond it, and superhuman
Silences, and profound quietude
In my thoughts I imagine, where almost
The heart doesn't fear. As the wind
Is heard rustling through these plants. I, that
Infinite silence to this voice
Go comparing: and I'm reminded of the eternal,
And the dead seasons, and the present
And the living, and her sound. So within this
Infinity, my thoughts drown
And sinking2 is sweet to me in this sea.
